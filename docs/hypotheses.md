## Hypothesis 1 — Guided, prioritised first steps with visible progress

We believe that a personalised Day 1 checklist with a visible progress tracker will help new starters know what to focus on and feel confident about what's done and what's next, because P02, P05, and P06 described being given equal-weight access to many systems with no prioritisation, and P03/P06 described finishing tasks without knowing their next priority.

[Feasibility: LOW — static checklist + progress bar, no backend needed. Strongest Week 3 slice candidate.]

## Hypothesis 2 — Centralised information / single source of truth

We believe that a single onboarding hub will reduce time spent searching, because all 6 participants independently described splitting their search across 3+ systems with no single source of truth, and 4 of 6 described real version-confusion incidents costing up to an hour each.

[Feasibility: MEDIUM–HIGH — needs a real document index/data model to be meaningful. Too broad for a Week 3 code slice; represent in Figma with static sample data only.]

## Hypothesis 3 — Trustworthy, contextual support (who to ask + transparent AI)

We believe that surfacing the right contact for each task, alongside AI answers that clearly cite sources and last-updated dates, will reduce time lost to uncertainty and build trust, because P02/P06 described not knowing who to ask, and all 6 participants independently said they'd only trust AI answers that showed where the information came from — with HR, salary, disciplinary, and legal matters always needing a human.

[Feasibility: LOW–MEDIUM for the "who to ask" half (static contact list, buildable). MEDIUM for the AI-trust half (no real AI needed — a UI-only mockup showing "source: [doc], updated [date]" is realistic and cheap). Good Week 3 slice candidate for the contextual-help half; AI-trust half is a stretch goal.]

## Hypothesis 4 — Role-specific content over generic training

We believe that role-specific onboarding content will feel more useful than generic training, because P02, P06, and P07 all separately described mandatory compliance training as necessary but disconnected from their actual day-to-day work.

[Feasibility: MEDIUM–HIGH — needs real content variation per role, ideally driven by a data structure. Mock with 1–2 hardcoded role variants for a demo rather than a full system.]
